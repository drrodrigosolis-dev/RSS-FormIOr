Page 1: Why FormIOr Exists

Home | Next: Core Concepts

FormIOr exists to solve a common problem: FormIO data is powerful, but the structure can be difficult for non-technical teams to work with. Exports often contain nested lists, repeated sections, and multiple rows per submission. The result is that simple reporting tasks take far too long or become inconsistent across teams.

FormIOr provides a repeatable path from raw responses to clean, analysis-ready outputs. It is designed for teams who need to share data with program leads, analysts, or partners who may only be comfortable with Excel. That is why the package focuses on clear naming, readable codebooks, and a multi-sheet export workbook that captures both raw and cleaned data.

A second goal is transparency. When data is cleaned or transformed, those changes should be recorded. FormIOr includes audit logging and a wizard that saves the exact steps taken. This makes it easier to explain and reproduce results over time.

This package and handbook are maintained by Rodrigo Solis-Sosa, PhD, Human Dimensions of Wildlife Specialist, BC Ministry of Water, Land and Resource Stewardship.

Key design goals:

  • Make FormIO data usable by non-technical staff
  • Encourage repeatable, documented workflows
  • Reduce errors caused by manual copy/paste steps
  • Provide outputs that are safe to share

If your team needs to produce consistent monthly or quarterly reports from FormIO, FormIOr is meant to be the “one workflow” you can return to.
